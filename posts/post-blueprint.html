Special Relativity

In special relativity, time and space are not absolute, but relative depending on the observer's frame of reference. The Lorentz transformation equations describe how measurements of space and time differ between two inertial frames.

The time dilation formula is given by:

\[ t' = \frac{t}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} \]

where:

  • \( t' \) is the time interval in the moving frame,
  • \( t \) is the time interval in the stationary frame,
  • \( v \) is the relative velocity between the frames, and
  • \( c \) is the speed of light.

Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics is the branch of physics that describes the behavior of particles at microscopic scales, including wave-particle duality, uncertainty principle, and quantum entanglement.

The Schrödinger equation:

\[ i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \Psi = \hat{H} \Psi \]

where:

  • \( \Psi \) is the wave function,
  • \( \hbar \) is the reduced Planck's constant,
  • \( \hat{H} \) is the Hamiltonian operator.
